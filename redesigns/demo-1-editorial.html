The morning we left London Heathrow with one-way tickets to Bangkok we had no real plan beyond a vague easterly direction and a quiet conviction that the long, retirement-deferred life of the West was not the only one on offer to us. We told the boys we would show them, rather than tell them — that there is more than one way to live, and more than one way to thrive.

Fourteen months in, the questions have changed. The boys are no longer asking when we are going home. They are asking how the buffalo know to come down to the water in the heat of the day. They are asking why the kumquats arrive on scooters tied with rubber bands. They are asking what the writing on the bunting means — and answering, sometimes, before we can.

Each piece on this site is a dispatch, a single observation worked over slowly until it becomes a small essay. We publish on Saturdays. We invite you, if any of this is of interest, to read along.
