Why we went, and why we kept going.

We are J, R, E and e — one family from the UK. We recognised the limits that mainstream schooling has for our neurodiverse boys, sold most of what we owned, strapped on four backpacks and booked one-way tickets from Heathrow to Bangkok.

The West's bargain — work until you retire, then live the life you wanted — assumes a perfect health and a surviving pension we are not prepared to bet our family on. So we are showing the boys, rather than telling them, that there is more than one way to live. And more than one way to thrive.
